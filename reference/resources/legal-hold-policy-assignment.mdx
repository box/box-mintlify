Legal Hold Assignments are used to assign Legal Hold
Policies to Users, Folders, Files, or File Versions.

Creating a Legal Hold Assignment puts a hold
on the File-Versions that belong to the Assignment's
'apply-to' entity.

* [Standard](/reference/resources/legal-hold-policy-assignment/)
* [Base](/reference/resources/legal-hold-policy-assignment--base/)

<Info>
This resource has a few variations that can be encountered when
using the API.

The fields that are part of the **base** variant represent
the default fields that are always returned when using the `fields`
query parameter, additional to the fields specified in the
parameter.
</Info>

<ParamField body="id" type="string">
example: `11446498`

The unique identifier for this legal hold assignment.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="type" type="string">
example: `legal_hold_policy_assignment`

The value will always be `legal_hold_policy_assignment`.

Value is always `legal_hold_policy_assignment`
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="assigned_at" type="string(date-time)">
example: `2012-12-12T10:53:43-08:00`

When the legal hold policy assignment object was
created.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="assigned_by" type="User (Mini)object">
The user who created the legal hold policy
assignment.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="assigned_to" type="File/Folder/Web linkobject">
The item that the legal hold policy
is assigned to. Includes type and ID.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="deleted_at" type="string(date-time)">
example: `2012-12-12T10:53:43-08:00`

When the assignment release request was sent.
(Because it can take time for an assignment to fully
delete, this isn't quite the same time that the
assignment is fully deleted). If null, Assignment
was not deleted.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="legal_hold_policy" type="Legal hold policy (Mini)object">
The policy that the legal hold policy assignment
is part of.
</ParamField>
